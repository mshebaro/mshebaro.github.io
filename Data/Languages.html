English

The Lebanese Education System played a tremendous role in enforcing immersive foreign language learning in both schools and universities. English has been set as the default language of instruction for several subjects. Other leading factors that have expedited my English grasping process are playing video games with native speakers of that language as well as watching a lot of shows in the Cartoon Network channel growing up (Ed Edn Eddy was a favorite).

Arabic

Lebanon is one of the 22 states of the Arab League. Arabic is embodied in 3 forms: Classical, Modern, and Colloquial Arabic. Classical Arabic can be found in old scriptures and poems. Modern Arabic is the standardized written form among all Arabs. It is taught in the academia and is used in media and law. Colloquial Arabic is classified into a multitude of spoken dialects. In my case, my dialect is Levantine Arabic.

French

My basic knowledge in French is chalked up to classes I took in high school, acquiring the conjugations using Duolingo, and the influence of the French Mandate on our mother tongue. For example, we would regularly use french words such as "eau de Javel" to refer to bleach, "ascenseur" to refer to an elevator,"disjoncteur" to refer to a circuit breaker, and "cadeau" to refer to a gift.
